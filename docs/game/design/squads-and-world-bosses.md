# Squads and world bosses

Squads are the intended unit of high-risk play. Boss rewards compare squads with squads so support and coordination remain valuable.

## Squads

- Maximum size is **four**, enough to cover damage, control, support, and guard roles without losing coordination.
- Kill credit goes to the player or unit that dealt the most damage. Last-hit credit is too gameable and encourages theft.
- A squad chooses **free-for-all** or **shared** loot in a safe zone. Shared loot splits materials among nearby members; free-for-all drops them with 30-second squad priority. The rule cannot change in the field.

The priority window protects a squad from outsiders, not teammates from each other.

## World bosses

Bosses are contested open-world flashpoints. Multiple squads fight the biome-themed boss and each other. Danger determines difficulty; rim bosses are the main source of heavy materials.

Contribution is pooled by squad. Personal rankings would make teammates compete and punish support; pooling makes the contest occur between squads and soloists.

| Reward | Eligibility | Distribution |
|---|---|---|
| Signature materials | Top four contributing squads or soloists | Split by contribution ratio |
| Participation | Everyone above threshold | Base materials, XP, currency |

Rim bosses are the only source of [Signature-tier](progression-and-crafting.md#rarity-tiers) materials, which makes them the gate on the last third of the [vertical budget](progression-and-crafting.md#the-vertical-budget). That is deliberate: the strongest gear is earned in the most contested, most cooperative content, and it is a slow item-by-item climb rather than a single unlock.

The participation threshold is a tunable percentage of boss health, not a flat value, so it scales and cannot be earned with one trivial hit.

Coordinated squads will usually claim the rare slots; that is consistent with cooperative progression. Meaningful solo contribution still receives a floor reward.

# Design pillars

These commitments are ordered. When two desirable features conflict, the earlier pillar wins.

## P1 — Skill and tactics beat levels

A level-20 player can defeat a level-80 player through aim, movement, prediction, positioning, or the right tactical answer. Levels buy options and skill ceiling, never decisive statistical advantage. This is SpellFire's identity and its hardest constraint.

## P2 — Vertical progression stays compressed

Core combat stats remain within a narrow band so [raw time-to-kill](combat.md#time-to-kill) stays stable across levels. Progression grants weapons, spells, builds, and counters—not dominant numbers. A maxed character has more answers, not more power.

## P3 — Cooperation is the intended strategy

Squads outperform soloists in the most valuable areas. Exceptional players may solo dangerous content, but grouping remains optimal. Difficulty, rewards, and the economy should encourage cooperation without taxing it.

## P4 — The world is seamless and expandable

SpellFire has one contiguous world, not instanced zones. It must grow without rebuilding existing content; danger therefore rises outward from a safe center. See [`world.md`](world.md).

## Resolved tension: progression without power creep

“Stronger” always means greater choice, commitment, or skill ceiling within the same effective power band. It never means an unanswerable number increase. This interpretation governs every weapon, spell tier, crafted component, and unlock; [`invariants.md`](invariants.md) records the resulting rules.
